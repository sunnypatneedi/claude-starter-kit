## Pitch Deck Structure

### The 10-Slide Framework

```
1. TITLE: Company name, tagline, your name
2. PROBLEM: The pain you're solving (specific, relatable)
3. SOLUTION: How you solve it (simple, clear)
4. WHY NOW: Market timing, trends enabling this
5. MARKET: TAM/SAM/SOM with bottom-up logic
6. PRODUCT: Demo or key screenshots
7. TRACTION: Metrics, growth, proof points
8. BUSINESS MODEL: How you make money
9. TEAM: Why you're the ones to do this
10. ASK: How much, what you'll do with it
```

### Slide-by-Slide Guide

**Slide 1: Title**

```
INCLUDE:
├── Company name and logo
├── One-line description
├── Your name and title
├── Contact info
└── [Optional] Impressive metric or credential
```

**Slide 2: Problem**

```
STRUCTURE:
├── Story or statistic that creates urgency
├── Who has this problem
├── How big/painful is it
├── Why existing solutions fail
└── NO mention of your product yet

TIPS:
- Make it emotional, not just rational
- Use specific numbers
- "X million people struggle with..."
- "Companies waste $X billion on..."
```

**Slide 3: Solution**

```
STRUCTURE:
├── Your solution in one sentence
├── How it works (simple, 3-step max)
├── Why it's 10x better
└── Clear before/after

TIPS:
- Don't oversell features
- Focus on outcome, not technology
- "We do [X] so that [Y]"
```

**Slide 4: Why Now**

```
DRIVERS:
├── Technology shift (what's newly possible)
├── Market shift (what's changed)
├── Behavior shift (how people act now)
├── Regulatory shift (what's allowed)
└── Why this couldn't exist 5 years ago

THIS SLIDE ANSWERS:
- Why hasn't someone done this before?
- Why will this work now?
```

**Slide 5: Market Size**

```
TAM: Total Addressable Market
├── Everyone who could theoretically buy

SAM: Serviceable Addressable Market
├── The segment you can reach

SOM: Serviceable Obtainable Market
├── What you can realistically capture in 3-5 years

BUILD BOTTOM-UP:
├── # of target customers × average revenue per customer
├── NOT top-down percentage of giant market
```

**Slide 6: Product**

```
OPTIONS:
├── Live demo (risky but powerful)
├── Recorded demo video
├── Key screenshots with callouts
├── Before/after comparison

FOCUS ON:
├── The "aha moment"
├── User experience, not features
├── Proof it works
```

**Slide 7: Traction**

```
METRICS BY STAGE:

Pre-launch:
├── Waitlist signups
├── LOIs or pilot commitments
├── Advisor commitments

Early stage:
├── Users/customers
├── Growth rate (MoM)
├── Engagement metrics
├── Revenue (even if small)

Growth stage:
├── ARR/MRR
├── Growth rate
├── Unit economics (CAC, LTV)
├── Net revenue retention
```

**Slide 8: Business Model**

```
INCLUDE:
├── How you charge (model)
├── Price points
├── Key metrics (margins, LTV, CAC)
├── Why this model makes sense

SHOW:
├── Path to profitability
├── Scalability of model
├── Defensibility
```

**Slide 9: Team**

```
INCLUDE:
├── Founders with photos
├── Relevant experience (10 words max per person)
├── Domain expertise
├── [Optional] Key advisors

HIGHLIGHT:
├── Why you specifically
├── Previous wins
├── Unique unfair advantage
├── Complementary skills
```

**Slide 10: Ask**

```
INCLUDE:
├── How much raising
├── What round (Seed, Series A, etc.)
├── Use of funds (3-4 buckets)
├── Milestones this unlocks
├── [Optional] Who's already in

EXAMPLE:
"Raising $2M Seed
├── 40% Engineering
├── 30% Go-to-market
├── 20% Operations
├── 10% Reserve

This gets us to: [Milestone]"
```

## Pitch Storytelling Framework

### The Narrative Arc

```
1. SETUP: The world before
   "For years, [audience] has struggled with [problem]..."

2. CONFLICT: The villain
   "The current solutions [fail because]..."

3. HERO: Your solution
   "We built [product] to [solve this]..."

4. PROOF: Evidence it works
   "Already, [traction/results]..."

5. VISION: The future
   "Imagine a world where [outcome]..."

6. CALL: Join us
   "We're raising [X] to make this real..."
```

### Opening Hook Options

```
PROBLEM HOOK:
"Every day, X million people [suffer from problem]..."

STATISTIC HOOK:
"$X billion is wasted annually on [inefficiency]..."

PERSONAL HOOK:
"When I was [experience], I discovered [insight]..."

QUESTION HOOK:
"What if you could [dream outcome]?"

CONTRAST HOOK:
"[Industry] has changed everything except [your area]..."
```

## Investor Meeting Flow

### 30-Minute Pitch Meeting

```
0-2 min: Rapport building
2-5 min: Your background, company overview
5-10 min: Problem and solution
10-15 min: Market and traction
15-20 min: Business model and team
20-25 min: Q&A
25-30 min: Ask and next steps
```

### Common Investor Questions

**Market**

```
- How big is this market really?
- How did you calculate TAM?
- Who's the competition?
- What's your moat?
```

**Traction**

```
- What's your growth rate?
- What's driving growth?
- What are your unit economics?
- What's your retention look like?
```

**Team**

```
- Why are you the right team?
- How do you know each other?
- What's your unfair advantage?
- Who's missing from the team?
```

**Business**

```
- How do you make money?
- What's the path to profitability?
- What are the biggest risks?
- What happens if you run out of money?
```

### Handling Tough Questions

```
STRUCTURE: Acknowledge → Answer → Bridge

"That's a great question..."
├── [Direct answer - don't dodge]
├── [Context if needed]
└── [Bridge to strength]

EXAMPLES:

Q: "You don't have experience in this industry"
A: "You're right that we're outsiders to [industry]. What we bring is [relevant experience]. And actually, being outsiders has let us see [insight] that incumbents miss."

Q: "Your competition is huge"
A: "Yes, [competitor] is well-established. They're focused on [X] while we're going after [Y]. We've actually won deals against them by [differentiator]."

Q: "Your numbers are small"
A: "We're early, absolutely. What's exciting is the trajectory - we've grown X% month over month. At this rate, we'll hit [milestone] by [date]."
```

## Pitch Practice Checklist

```markdown
## Pitch Practice: [Date]

### Before Practicing

- [ ] Know your 3 key messages
- [ ] Have data memorized (don't read slides)
- [ ] Anticipate top 10 questions
- [ ] Prepare 3 stories/examples

### During Practice

- [ ] Time the full pitch (aim for 15-20 min without Q&A)
- [ ] Record yourself
- [ ] Practice with friendly audience first
- [ ] Get feedback on unclear parts

### Delivery Checklist

- [ ] Strong opening (first 30 seconds hook)
- [ ] Eye contact, not reading slides
- [ ] Varied pace and energy
- [ ] Pauses for emphasis
- [ ] Clear transitions between sections
- [ ] Confident close with specific ask

### Content Review

- [ ] Every slide has one main point
- [ ] Minimal text (6 words max per bullet)
- [ ] Data is credible and sourced
- [ ] Story is coherent beginning to end
- [ ] No jargon or unexplained acronyms
```

## Pitch Red Flags to Avoid

```
CONTENT:
├── Claiming "no competition"
├── TAM built top-down (% of huge market)
├── Hand-wavy unit economics
├── Feature dump without outcomes
├── No clear differentiation

DELIVERY:
├── Reading slides word-for-word
├── Going over time
├── Getting defensive at questions
├── Not knowing your numbers
├── Lacking passion/conviction

DECK:
├── Too many slides (>15)
├── Walls of text
├── Blurry screenshots
├── Inconsistent design
├── Outdated numbers
```
