## Core Frameworks

### The Reflection Cycle

```
EXPERIENCE → REFLECT → LEARN → APPLY → EXPERIENCE

1. What happened?
2. What did I think/feel?
3. What does this mean?
4. What will I do differently?
```

### Levels of Reflection

```
Level 1: WHAT - Describe what happened
Level 2: SO WHAT - Analyze meaning and impact
Level 3: NOW WHAT - Plan future action
```

## Journaling Prompts by Situation

### When Feeling Stuck

- What would I do if I knew I couldn't fail?
- What advice would I give a friend in this situation?
- What's the smallest next step I could take?
- What am I afraid of?

### When Facing a Decision

- What are the real options here?
- What would I regret not doing?
- What does my gut say?
- How will I feel about this in 10 years?

### When Processing Emotions

- What am I actually feeling right now?
- Where do I feel this in my body?
- What triggered this feeling?
- What does this feeling need from me?

### When Celebrating Success

- What specifically contributed to this win?
- Who helped me get here?
- What can I learn from how this worked?
- How can I build on this momentum?

### When Learning from Failure

- What actually happened vs. what I expected?
- What was in my control? What wasn't?
- What would I do differently?
- What's the silver lining or lesson here?

## Reflection Practices

### Morning Pages

- Write 3 pages stream-of-consciousness
- No editing, no judgment
- First thing in the morning
- Clears mental clutter

### Gratitude Practice

- List 3 specific things daily
- Include why you're grateful
- Vary the categories

### Five-Minute Journal

- Morning: Today I'm grateful for... Today will be great if... Affirmation
- Evening: Amazing things that happened... How could today have been better?

## Output Format

When guiding reflection:

1. **Appropriate prompt**: Based on situation
2. **Framework**: Structure to organize thoughts
3. **Follow-up questions**: To go deeper
4. **Action item**: What to do with the insight
